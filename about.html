Company Background

Digital Radio Plus has been operating for 20 years, broadcasting channels using the highly popular DAB+ transmission technology. The company started as a small radio studio, operating in regional Australia. Over the years, the radio studio has passed through a number of transformations.

As the reputation of the studio grew nationally, and production expanded to meet the popular demand, the structure of the business changed to its present form. The production staff increased and currently includes 3 production managers (programme controllers), 30 full-time staff including composers, editors, journalists, presenters, and sound engineers.

Transmission Technologies

DAB+ is the most widely adopted digital radio standard worldwide, on-air in more than 40 countries including Australia. DAB+ uses an advanced and technically robust transmission system and operates in Australia in VHF Band III. Like other digital technologies, it uses higher frequencies which work extremely well within the coverage area but have a "cliff edge" drop off at the boundary of reception.

DAB+ is both a cost-effective and spectrum-efficient technology, allowing broadcasters to offer their popular analogue stations simulcast in digital quality, and significant choice from new DAB+ only station formats such as kids, dance, sport, urban, hard and heavy, chill, and smooth. DAB+ uses a robust modulation designed for radio reception in mobile environments, such as vehicles and public transport.
